About

Susanna Johnson is a graduate of the Indiana University Jacobs School of Music, where she studied viola performance with Atar Arad. While pursuing her degree, Susanna studied violin and viola pedagogy with Mimi Zweig and Brenda Brenner and taught as part of the world-renowned Indiana University String Academy. Susanna is an avid enthusiast of teaching young children. In the spring of 2011 Susanna served as a clinician for the Santa Fe Youth Symphony Association Elementary String Program, offering both private instruction and masterclasses. During her final year at Indiana University, Susanna worked as a violin instructor for the Jacobs School of Music’s Fairview Elementary violin project, a program focusing on bringing music education to children of low socio-economic households. This past year, Susanna volunteered twice as part of a collaboration between the non-profit organization MusAid and the Secretary of Culture of El Salvador’s El Sistema, teaching children from many regions of El Salvador and providing viola teachers new insight on viola pedagogy. In the future, Susanna hopes to broaden her connections with Central and South America and continue to help the spread of music education there. In addition, Susanna hopes to expand her pedagogical training and studies, to the aim of innovating the way the instrument is taught. Susanna is working on a Master’s degree in viola performance at the University of Maryland where she studies with Katherine Murdock.
