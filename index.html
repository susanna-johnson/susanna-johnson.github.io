About


Susanna has a Master’s degree in viola performance at the University of Maryland and a Bachelor of Music in viola performance at the Indiana University Jacobs School of Music. Susanna studied for a year at Boston Conservatory, as part of the Graduate Performer’s Diploma program. During her studies at Boston Conservatory, Susanna performed as Principal viola for the Boston Conservatory Orchestra as well as a section player in the Orchestra at Indian Hill, the Harvard-Radcliffe Orchestra, the Palaver Strings Ensemble and the Cape Anne Symphony. As a graduate assistant at the University of Maryland, Susanna taught viola technique class as well as coached viola sectionals, and performed as Principal viola for the University of Maryland Symphony Orchestra. During her studies at University of Maryland, Susanna performed as part of the University of Maryland Conductor’s Lab Orchestra, the Peacherine Ragtime Orchestra and the Wesley Choral Festival Orchestra.

As a chamber musician, Susanna has been selected to perform in many honors chamber performances such as the University of Maryland chamber honor’s recitals, the Montecito International Music Festival Gala concert, and the Tennessee Tuesday luncheon for the Tennessee United States Senators in Washington, D.C., among other performances. Her chamber music coaches have included Peter Takacs, Peter Stumpf, Shigeo Neriki and Ik-Hwan Bae. Susanna has also proven her worth as a competitive soloist: she has been selected as honorable mention at the string division semifinals for the University of Maryland concerto competition, as a finalist for the concerto competition at the National Symphony Orchestra Summer Music Institute, and as First Place Junior Division at the Bach Fest Competition in Rutherford County, Tennessee. Susanna has also performed for Masterclasses with Larry Dutton, Roberto Diaz, Doris Lederer and Yizhak Schotten.

Teaching

Susanna is an avid enthusiast of teaching music to children and adults and has extensive pedagogical training. In 2021, Susanna completed her Post-Baccalaureate at Gordon College in Music Education, where she studied instrumental and general music methods including voice, brass, winds, guitar, conducting and world music. Between 2016-2021, Susanna taught Kindergarten and Elementary string ensemble classes, general music and private lessons at Bridge Boston Charter School in the position of Lower School Music Specialist. Susanna’s string instrument pedagogical training includes string pedagogy with Mimi Zweig, Brenda Brenner and Rebecca Henry, and Suzuki method (more about the Suzuki method here) certifications in books 1 & 2. Susanna follows the El Sistema music education philosophy to use music education as a catalyst for community, belonging and social change.

In addition to her work at Bridge Boston Charter School, Susanna taught at South Shore Conservatory as Suzuki Violin Teacher between 2015-2018, specializing in beginner violin lessons for children between the ages of 4 and 6. Always looking to expand her knowledge and skills, Susanna traveled to Sweden twice to study with El Sistema mentor Ron Alvarez, once as part of the El Sistema Europe Academy 2019 and again in 2020 to study the Dream Orchestra, an orchestra for refugees in Gothenburg. In Spring of 2021, Susanna took an Introductory Course on the Dalcroze Method at the Dalcroze Music School of Boston. In 2020, to better reach her students through online teaching, Susanna started her Youtube Channel: Music with Ms. Johnson, a channel with educational videos to teach children music through games, songs and stories. Currently, Susanna lives in Iași, Romania.

Ms. Susanna, as she was known in our house, was our son's first violin teacher - and she was outstanding. Savi, who has some learning issues, needed a patient teacher, one who could tailor her teaching to his attentional needs and to what he would find interesting. Susanna was firm when she needed to be, but engaged with him on the subjects that he was interested in, like Star Wars and Harry Potter, even making up a "Darth Vader" song for him to play on the violin as a way of motivating him.

As a parent, we loved Susanna because she kept us informed of what was happening in the lessons and took time at the end of each lesson to show us what she taught him and what he needed to practice and how so that we could help him develop his skills.

The payoff to working with Susanna was huge. In his school recital at the end of the year, Savi was the best string player in the bunch and his joy in playing the violin is clear. He just got back from a month at sleep away camp and the first thing he did when he got home was reach for his violin and play Song with the Wind.

I would recommend Susanna without hesitation! If Savi's future teacher's are half as good, I'll consider myself truly fortunate.

Ariel Roth (parent)
